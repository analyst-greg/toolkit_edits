Salary research for prospect dev roles curated from LinkedIn, public databases, and more.

Overview

This is exactly what it sounds like: a little snapshot of industry data, independently curated. I’m not trying to compete with the Apra International Salary Survey, and I doubt any of my findings will be all that surprising to those who have read any of the 100+ pages of that report.

The Good News

The data I have gathered is free to explore and download - instead of being locked down in a PDF. Also, I don’t have the 3.5% margin of error the Apra Salary Survey does, because I’m avoiding self-report data entirely, except as a means for comparison.

The Bad News

If you want stronger methodology, more comprehensive data (e.g. demographics), broader scope, and mild abuse of the “3d” effect for bar charts…you’ll have to wait until the next Apra Salary Survey comes out in 2027.1 I limited myself to public information, which as any prospect researcher will tell you, can sometimes be prone to massive gaps.

The Prospect Dev Salary Dataset

The first attempt at cobbling this dataset together yielded:

  • 1507 individuals, with varying levels of missing information, but that can be enriched over time
  • 152 jobs tied to individuals, with salary information from 2017-2025
  • 84 job listings, between 2024-2026, including descriptions, some salary information, and number of applicants as of the date researched

The job listings dataset will be analyzed and published later, though I’m including some of the salary highlights in this post.

I focused on public university employees, since their salaries are easy to find via government websites.

Bulk Data

I made a data request to the National Labor Exchange (NLx) Research Hub, to try and get access to a larger corpus of online job adds, occupation codes, tools, salaries, etc. However, I don’t think my research case is as compelling as the guys at Loyola who trained an entire model on billions of data points to make very slick job analysis modules in Python. Luckily, their Job Ad Analysis Toolkit will help me with my unstructured text problem - which has been building up as I grab job descriptions and other data from LinkedIn. However, their giant corpus, as far as I know, is under an agreement with NLx that may specify what they can or can’t publish.

I need the job-level data so I can filter for prospect dev roles, and I’m tempted to email them to ask for a subset of their corpus, but again, the NLx agreement may prevent that.

National bulk data for prospect dev roles remains out of reach, for now, so I had to do a state-by-state approach, starting with large public universities.
